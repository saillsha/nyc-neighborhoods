Overview

The building floor plan for the Free People BUFFALO, NY Walden Galleria location is marked up with one distinct zone. Nomi recommends a one-sensor installation to cover the proposed zone plus one camera door counter. This includes one wired sensor for zone 1. The proposed installation indicates that the wired sensor be installed in a convenient location at the cash wrap, as indicated in Diagram 1. In the event that there is not an available pre-wired Ethernet drop at the cash wrap to the network rack/closet, a single gateway sensor has been included that will be the hard-wired sensor, allowing the zone sensor to mesh with the gateway sensor.

The camera door counter is to be installed in a convenient location at least 9 ft above the entrance doors in the open ceiling, as indicated in Diagram 1. The camera will be adjusted via the camera interface after installation, so the exact location of the camera over the door is not critical. A single Ethernet cable pull will be required from the network rack to the camera location.

All equipment is shipped to location with each device preconfigured and clearly labeled.

Scope of Work/Schedule

Install and configure Nomi sensors and cameras. The scheduled installation date is October 10, 2013 at 7AM. The installation will be performed by Spencer Technologies on-site with on-site support from the Nomi customer support center. The installation is expected to take three hours and the initial configuration and machine learning process will take two weeks before the store is live.

For further instruction on how to point and zoom the camera view, please refer to Axis User Manual - Axis M3204 at:

    http://www.axis.com/files/manuals/um_m3204_45313_en_1112.pdf

    In order to view the camera for aiming, you must connect the camera to your laptop. To do this:
    • Connect a single Ethernet cable from the camera’s Ethernet port to the “Data + Power Out” port on the Camera PoE Injector, designated by , and then another Ethernet cable from the Camera PoE Injector’s “Data In” port to the laptop’s Ethernet port. Power up the Camera PoE Injector.
    • Ensure that your laptop’s wifi is disabled/turned off, that you are not Bluetooth tethered to your cell phone or any other device, and that you do not have a static IP address set on your Ethernet LAN adapter interface.
    • Launch the IP Utility. When the utility runs, it will find the camera and display the Name, IP Address, and Serial Number in the Utility’s table. Double- click the IP address in the table and it will launch your browser and connect you to the camera.
    • You should be prompted for a username and password, which are “root” and “password” respectively, without the quotation marks.
    • Once logged into the camera, if you do not see video, you may see a yellow bar across the top of your browser window that will ask you to install an ActiveX control. You MUST install this control in order to see the video.
    • When you see the video, make the necessary adjustments to aim the camera as needed and focus the camera to the floor.
    • Once aimed, zoomed, and focused, tighten all the adjustment lockdown screws so that the aiming doesn’t change, and verify that it is still aimed correctly.
    • Disconnect the temporary cable from the camera and connect the Ethernet drop from the network switch.
    Adjust the black plastic shield inside the camera cover so that the opening will let the lens of the camera see through it, and attach the cover to the camera with the supplied screws
